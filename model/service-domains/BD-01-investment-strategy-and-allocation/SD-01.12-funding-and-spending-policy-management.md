# SD-01.12 — Funding & Spending Policy Management

**Business Domain:** BD-01 Investment Strategy & Allocation (Front office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Governs the policy by which capital flows *into* and *out of* the fund — the rule that sets the size of the pool BD-01's allocation is exercised over. For a DB pension this is the contribution policy, the funding strategy and the recovery plan; for an endowment or foundation it is the spending rule — constant-growth, moving-average or hybrid; for a sovereign wealth fund it is the governed inflow and withdrawal rule. SD-01.12 also manages the ongoing relationship with the party that sets those flows — the plan sponsor, the endowed institution, the state — because the funding and spending policy is negotiated and reviewed with the sponsor, not set unilaterally by the investment function.

This is an **asset-owner capability** — it has no asset-manager analogue, because a manager never funds itself or sets its own spending rule. It is distinct from SD-01.2 Investment Mandate & Policy Definition: SD-01.2 codifies what a pool of capital is run *to* — objectives, risk appetite, constraints — at a point in time; SD-01.12 governs how much capital flows in and out, on what governed rule. The funding level and spending rule it produces are inputs to allocation: SD-01.4 Strategic Asset Allocation sizes the return objective against the spending draw or the funding gap, and SD-01.11 Liquidity Strategy & Tiering plans for the cash the policy obliges. It is core for pension funds, sovereign wealth funds and endowment-style asset owners, partial for an insurer, and dormant for an asset manager, wealth manager or hedge fund. Where an asset owner delegates to an OCIO or fiduciary manager, SD-01.12 is one of the Service Domains the OCIO operates on the client's behalf — covenant monitoring in particular is commonly delegated; the OCIO's authority to do so is set by the `inbound-delegated` mandate codified in SD-01.2, and SD-01.12 remains the owner of the funding and spending policy.

## Service Operations

- **Set and review the funding policy** — for a DB pension, the contribution schedule, the funding target and the recovery plan that closes a deficit over a governed horizon.
- **Set and review the spending or withdrawal rule** — for an endowment, foundation or sovereign fund, the spending rule (constant-growth, moving-average, hybrid) or the governed inflow and withdrawal rule, and the periodic recalibration of its rate.
- **Monitor the sponsor covenant and relationship** — assess the employer covenant standing behind a DB pension, and manage the wider sponsor relationship — the state, the plan sponsor, the endowed institution — through which funding and spending are agreed.
- **Operate the funding-and-spending governance cycle** — run the periodic review at which the funding level is struck, the spending rate is set and the policy is re-agreed with the sponsor and the governing bodies.
- **Feed the funding level and spending rule to allocation and liquidity** — pass the funded status, the contribution or spending draw and the recovery horizon to SD-01.4 and SD-01.11 as binding inputs to the allocation and liquidity decisions.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the actuarial valuation and funded status; the liability profile and benefit projections; the sponsor's financial standing and covenant assessment; the endowed institution's spending need or the sovereign fund's inflow and withdrawal mandate; the regulatory funding regime.
- **Outputs:** the funding policy, the spending or withdrawal rule, the recovery plan and the covenant assessment — consumed by **SD-01.4** (the return objective and funding gap), **SD-01.11** (the cash obligation to be funded), **SD-16.1** Corporate & Fund Governance (policy approval) and **SD-16.2** Owner & Investor Reporting (the funded-status and policy outcomes reported to the sponsor).

## Entities

- **Consumes:** Portfolio / Mandate (E-03) and the reference and book-of-record entities — Valuation (E-07, any `method`), Holding / Position (E-04, `book = abor`) — for the funded status.
- **Owns:** none — the funding policy, the spending rule and the covenant assessment are governed-policy artefacts. A candidate **Funding Policy** entity is an open extension.

## Standards

- The **Santiago Principles** (the Generally Accepted Principles and Practices, GAPP) — the sovereign-wealth-fund disclosure principles covering funding source, withdrawal rules and the spending framework.
- The **DB pension funding regime** — the UK DB Funding and Investment Strategy regime and its statutory funding objective, the employer-covenant guidance, and the NASRA funding-policy objectives for US public plans.
- The **endowment spending-rule literature** — the constant-growth, moving-average and hybrid spending formulas and the intergenerational-equity framing.

## Open extensions

- The Funding Policy as an entity, carrying the spending rule, the recovery plan and the covenant assessment as facets.
- The boundary with SD-01.7 Liability-Driven & Cash-Flow-Driven Strategy — SD-01.12 sets the contribution policy that funds the liability; SD-01.7 frames the investment strategy around it.
- The covenant-assessment sub-model and its review cadence relative to the actuarial valuation cycle.
