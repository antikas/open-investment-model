# SD-04.9 — Exit & Realisation Management

**Business Domain:** BD-04 Direct & Co-Investment (Front office) · **Applies:** PRIV

## Purpose

Plans and executes the exit of a direct holding and the realisation of its value. Exit & Realisation closes the deal lifecycle: it decides when and how to sell, runs the sale, and accounts for the proceeds. Like the diligence and structuring domains, its operations branch by mode — the exit routes for a company, a real asset and a direct loan are different — but it is one realisation capability.

## Service Operations

- **Assess exit readiness and plan timing** — evaluate when the holding is ready to be realised and analyse the exit options.
- **Execute an equity exit** — for a company, the trade sale to a strategic buyer, the IPO, the secondary buyout, or the dividend recapitalisation.
- **Execute a real-asset exit** — for a real asset, the asset or portfolio sale, the sale-and-leaseback, the refinancing, or the concession hand-back at end of term; for a natural-resource asset, the timberland or farmland sale and the disposal of a depleted or harvested holding.
- **Execute a credit exit** — for a direct loan, the repayment at maturity, the refinancing, or the secondary loan sale.
- **Run the GP-led secondary route** — the move of an asset into a continuation vehicle.
- **Manage the sale process** — prepare vendor due diligence, run the process, and manage the buyer field.
- **Account for the realisation** — record the realised proceeds and the deal-level track record.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the matured holding and the value-creation outcome (SD-04.8 / SD-04.10 / SD-04.12); exit-market conditions.
- **Outputs:** the executed exit, the realised proceeds and the deal-level track record — feeding BD-09 Performance & Analytics and BD-12 Investment Operations & Servicing.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** Legal Entity (E-01), Instrument / Asset (E-02), Direct Real Asset (RA-01), Transaction (E-05) — the exit is recorded as a Transaction; the SD-04.10 matured holding and the value-creation outcome; the SD-04.12 matured holding and the value-creation outcome; the SD-04.8 matured holding and the value-creation outcome.
- **Owns:** none — the exit is recorded as a Transaction (E-05), owned by BD-12's operations domains.

## Standards

- No single external standard governs exit. The four equity-exit routes (trade sale, IPO, secondary buyout, dividend recapitalisation), the GP-led secondary / continuation fund, and vendor due diligence are practitioner conventions.

## Open extensions

- The boundary with BD-12 on the funds-flow and settlement mechanics of an exit.
