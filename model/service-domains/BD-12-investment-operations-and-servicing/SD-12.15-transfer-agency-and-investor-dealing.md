# SD-12.15 — Transfer Agency & Investor Dealing

**Business Domain:** BD-12 Investment Operations & Servicing (Back office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Maintains the fund investor register and processes the dealing of fund units — the subscriptions, redemptions and switches of an open-ended fund's investors. SD-12.15 is the transfer-agency capability: it keeps the register of who owns units in each fund, processes investor subscription, redemption and switch orders against the dealing cut-off and the struck NAV, settles the subscription and redemption cash, pays the distributions, and issues the contract notes and statements. Fund administration is fund accounting plus transfer agency; SD-12.15 is the transfer-agency half, the sibling of SD-12.9 Fund Accounting & NAV. It exists because the open-ended fund's investor dealing is a distinct operational discipline with no equivalent elsewhere in BD-12 — SD-12.8 processes the *closed-end* capital-call and distribution events; SD-12.15 processes the *open-ended* registered-fund investor dealing. It is **not** the commercial client relationship — that is BD-15's client-management cluster; SD-12.15 runs the processing engine and the register, BD-15 owns the relationship. As the transfer-agency half of fund administration, it follows the same activation switch as its sibling SD-12.9: an institution that operates no funds of its own — a pure investor that only invests its own or its clients' capital — leaves SD-12.15 dormant.

## Service Operations

- **Maintain the investor register** — keep the register of holders of each fund's units, and the ownership and transfer record.
- **Process subscriptions and redemptions** — process investor subscription, redemption and switch orders against the dealing cut-off and the struck NAV.
- **Settle investor cash** — settle the subscription and redemption cash, and reconcile the dealing cash.
- **Pay distributions** — calculate and pay the income and capital distributions to the fund's investors.
- **Issue contract notes and statements** — issue the deal confirmations, contract notes and periodic investor statements.
- **Apply dealing controls** — apply the anti-dilution-levy / swing-pricing inputs, the large-deal and dilution controls, and the dealing-suspension handling.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** investor dealing orders, from the BD-15 client channels and the platforms; the struck NAV from SD-12.9; the onboarded investors from SD-15.11; the fund terms (PM-10).
- **Outputs:** the updated investor register, the processed deals, the settled investor cash and the contract notes — consumed by SD-12.9 Fund Accounting & NAV (the units in issue), SD-12.1 IBOR and SD-12.2 ABOR (the cash and position effects), SD-12.10 Reconciliation, SD-15.14 Client & Investor Reporting and SD-14.3 Financial Crime Prevention (the monitoring of investor flows).

## Entities

- **Consumes:** PM-01 Fund & Vehicle, FO-01 Fund Product (the issued-fund product record — the dealing terms on FO-01, including dealing frequency, cut-off and pricing basis, govern order processing; the fund_product_id anchors the investor register), FO-02 Share / Unit Class (the class the investor is subscribing to or redeeming from — the class ISIN, `class_currency`, `distribution_policy`, `min_investment` and `investor_category` on FO-02 govern order eligibility and processing), FO-05 Fund Distribution Event (the pay-date distribution event owned by SD-12.7 — SD-12.15 reads FO-05 `distribution_per_unit` multiplied by the investor's `units_held` at the record date to calculate and pay the per-investor distribution cash on the pay date), FO-08 Service-Provider Appointment (reads the `role = transfer_agent` appointment row for each fund to identify the appointed transfer agent — relevant when the transfer-agency function is outsourced to a third-party provider and SD-12.15 operates in an oversight mode), PM-10 Fund Terms, E-01 Legal Entity (the investors), E-06 Cash Flow Event; the SD-12.9 struck NAV; the SD-15.11 onboarded investors; FO-07 Investor Tax Statement (from SD-17.4 — the investor tax statement of record that SD-12.15 includes in the investor's annual reporting pack alongside the investor account statement; the transfer agent furnishes the tax statement to the investor as part of the year-end investor communication); FO-09 Omnibus Account (reads FO-09 to identify which investor accounts in the register are omnibus accounts held by intermediaries, apply the appropriate dealing, confirmation and reporting treatment distinct from direct investor accounts, and route net subscription / redemption instructions to the relevant omnibus holder — the TA/dealing boundary between direct register investors and omnibus-held underlying investors); FO-11 ETF Creation Basket (reads the daily Portfolio Composition File when validating and accepting FO-10 creation/redemption orders — the basket referenced on each FO-10 must match the published FO-11 for that dealing date; SD-12.15 confirms the AP's submitted composition against the authoritative FO-11).
- **Owns:** FO-03 Investor Unitholding (the per-investor, per-share-class register of units held — the authoritative investor register position, updated at each dealing cycle); FO-04 Dealing Order (the subscription, redemption, transfer or switch instruction — the immutable event at which the investor register moves); FO-10 ETF Creation/Redemption Order (the primary-market in-specie dealing event in which an authorised participant creates or redeems ETF creation units — the transfer-agency function accepts, validates, and settles these orders); FO-12 ETF Authorised-Participant Agreement (the standing, dated record of a broker-dealer's right to create and redeem ETF creation units against a specific fund — the transfer-agency function establishes, maintains, and terminates AP agreements and validates incoming FO-10 orders against them).

## Standards

- The **UCITS / AIFMD** dealing and investor-register requirements.
- **ISO 20022** investment-fund messaging — the `setr` order and `semt` statement message families.
- The anti-dilution and **swing-pricing** conventions; AML / KYC at the transfer-agency layer (cross-reference SD-14.3).

## Open extensions

- The swing-pricing and dealing-control sub-model.
- Look-through from omnibus accounts to underlying investor positions within a platform or nominee structure.
- The switch-leg cross-reference pairing — linking the outgoing and incoming FO-04 legs of a class switch as a governed pair.
- The Service-Operation-level input/output contracts.
