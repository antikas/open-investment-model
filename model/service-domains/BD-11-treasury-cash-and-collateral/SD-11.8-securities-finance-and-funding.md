# SD-11.8 — Securities Finance & Funding

**Business Domain:** BD-11 Treasury, Cash & Collateral (Middle office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Runs the firm's funding and financing book — the use of repo, securities lending and credit facilities to fund the firm's investing, and the securities-lending programme that lends the funds' assets for incremental return. SD-11.8 owns the securities-finance capability on the **principal side**: the decision to run a securities-lending programme and its design, the operating-model choice (agent / principal / direct), the funding book and its maturity ladder, the choice of funding source, prime-brokerage funding, the negotiation and execution of the firm's own repo and securities-financing trades, the oversight of the lending agent, and the reinvestment of the cash collateral the programme generates. There is no separate front-office securities-financing desk in the model — the financing trade is treasury's act of running the funding book. SD-11.8 is **not** the operational loan book — loan open, recall and return processing, per-loan collateral and lending-revenue accounting are SD-12.13 Securities Lending Operations — and it is not the collateral-and-margin cycle against the financing, which is SD-11.4.

## Service Operations

- **Run the funding book** — manage the firm's use of repo, securities lending and credit lines as funding, and the maturity ladder of that funding.
- **Choose the funding source** — decide the cheapest and most appropriate source of funding for a given need, across the available instruments.
- **Negotiate and execute financing trades** — where the firm transacts a repo, reverse repo, stock loan or stock borrow direct, negotiate the counterparty, rate, fee and term and execute the trade; borrowing to cover a short or a settlement fail is a funding decision owned here.
- **Operate the securities-lending programme** — set the programme design, the lendable-pool policy and the operating model, lend the funds' assets for incremental return, and set the borrower and reinvestment-risk policy — directly or through a lending agent.
- **Reinvest cash collateral** — manage the reinvestment of the cash collateral the securities-lending programme generates, within the reinvestment-risk policy.
- **Manage prime-brokerage funding** — manage the financing relationship with prime brokers, including margin financing and the cost and terms of leverage.
- **Oversee the lending agent** — where securities lending is run through an agent, oversee the agent's performance and the programme's risk.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the funding need from SD-11.2 Liquidity Management; the firm's lendable inventory from the book of record (SD-12.1); the operational loan-book position from SD-12.13; the reinvestment-risk policy; prime-broker terms.
- **Outputs:** the funding book, the securities-lending programme position and the financing cost — consumed by SD-11.2, SD-11.5 Collateral Optimisation & Inventory Management (financing and collateral are linked), SD-07.3 Liquidity Risk Management, and treasury governance.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-04 Holding / Position (`book = ibor` — the live IBOR lendable inventory), E-01 Legal Entity (borrowers, prime brokers, lending agents), E-05 Transaction (the financing trades); the SD-11.2 funding need; the SD-12.1 firm's lendable inventory from the book of record; the SD-12.13 operational loan-book position.
- **Owns:** none as a master entity — the funding-book facilities are a Credit Facility entity candidate (shared with SD-11.6); the securities-lending positions are recorded as E-04 Holding / Position.

## Standards

- The securities-lending best-practice frameworks — the industry and GFOA guidance.
- The repo and securities-lending master agreements — the **GMRA** and **GMSLA**.
- **SEC Rule 2a-7** and money-market conventions for cash-collateral reinvestment.

## Open extensions

- The securities-lending-programme and cash-collateral-reinvestment sub-model.
- The Service-Operation-level input/output contracts.
