# SD-12.3 — Trade Confirmation & Matching

**Business Domain:** BD-12 Investment Operations & Servicing (Back office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Confirms and matches the economic and operational details of a trade with the counterparty or broker, so both sides agree what was traded before it moves to settlement. A trade is executed on the desk; before the firm will settle it, it must be *agreed* — quantity, price, settlement date, instrument and settlement instructions reconciled against the counterparty's record of the same trade. SD-12.3 is that agreement step. It exists as a distinct capability because an unconfirmed or mismatched trade settling is the most common cause of a settlement fail, and the matching step is operationally separate from both execution and settlement. It is **not** trade execution — that is SD-06.2 — and it is not settlement itself — that is SD-12.4; SD-12.3 sits between them, turning an executed trade into a settlement-ready, agreed one.

## Service Operations

- **Trade affirmation** — affirm the executed trade against the broker's or counterparty's terms on trade date, the first agreement that both sides booked the same trade.
- **Confirmation matching** — match the firm's trade record to the counterparty confirmation field by field — instrument, quantity, price, settlement date, settlement currency — and flag any mismatch.
- **Allegement resolution** — investigate and resolve allegements: trades a counterparty asserts against the firm that the firm has no matching record of.
- **Mismatch and discrepancy management** — work the queue of matched-with-difference and unmatched trades to resolution, escalating where the counterparty disputes a term.
- **Standing settlement instruction enrichment** — attach the correct standing settlement instruction (SSI) to each confirmed trade, so the downstream settlement instruction carries the right account-and-place detail.
- **Central matching / CTM submission** — submit and match trades through a central trade-matching utility where one is used, in place of bilateral confirmation exchange.
- **Confirmation status reporting** — report the confirmation and matching status of the day's trades, including the unmatched population that threatens the settlement cycle.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** executed and allocated trades from SD-06.2 and SD-06.5 (E-05, PB-04, PB-05); broker and counterparty confirmations; standing settlement instructions; the security and entity masters for enrichment.
- **Outputs:** confirmed, matched, settlement-ready trades — consumed by SD-12.4 Trade Settlement (which instructs settlement only on a matched trade); confirmation status — consumed by SD-12.10 Reconciliation, SD-14.1 Enterprise & Operational Risk Management, and the trading desk.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-05 Transaction (`transaction_type = trade`), PB-04 Execution, PB-05 Allocation; the SD-06.2 executed and allocated trades; the SD-06.5 executed and allocated trades.
- **Owns:** none — confirmation and matching is a *process state* on a Transaction, not a master entity in its own right. The matched / unmatched / affirmed status is an attribute of the trade as it moves through the lifecycle, held as Transaction status; whether the standing settlement instruction (SSI) should be a reference entity is flagged on PB-06.
- **Open question:** the SSI as a managed reference entity rather than an inline reference — relevant here (enrichment) and on SD-12.4 (settlement).

## Standards

- **ISO 20022** and the legacy **ISO 15022** — the messaging standards trade confirmations are exchanged on; the ISO 15022 MT515 / MT517 confirmation messages and their ISO 20022 successors.
- **SWIFT** — the network most bilateral confirmation messaging runs over.
- Central trade-matching utilities (e.g. an institutional central-matching service) provide the matching infrastructure many firms use in place of bilateral exchange; T+1 settlement cycles compress the window in which matching must complete.

## Open extensions

- The Service-Operation-level input/output contracts.
- The standing settlement instruction (SSI) as a reference entity.
- The matching-tolerance sub-model — which field-level differences are auto-resolved and which require investigation.
