# SD-13.11 — Document & Content Management

**Business Domain:** BD-13 Investment Data & Reporting (Cross-cutting — data) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Manages the institution's investment documents and content — the repository, the deal rooms, the indexing, the versioning and the retention. Much of what an institutional investor knows arrives and lives as documents: manager reports, capital-account statements, LPAs and side letters, IC papers, trade confirmations, prospectuses, research notes, board minutes. SD-13.11 is the Service Domain that *holds and governs* those documents as artefacts — it is the repository. It is distinct from SD-13.6 GP & Manager Report Ingestion, which *extracts structured data* from a document: SD-13.11 stores and governs the document; SD-13.6 mines it. A capital-account statement lives in SD-13.11's repository, is registered with its metadata here, and is read by SD-13.6 there.

## Service Operations

- **Document repository management** — operate the document store: ingest documents, hold them securely, and make them retrievable.
- **Document metadata registration** — register each document with its structured metadata — what it is, when it was filed, the period it is *as of*, what durable record it concerns, where it came from — so it is discoverable and provenance-linked.
- **Document indexing and search** — index document content and metadata so a document can be found by what it is about, not only by where it was filed.
- **Deal-room management** — operate the secure data rooms for live transactions — provisioning access, organising diligence materials, tracking who has seen what — supporting BD-03 and BD-04 diligence.
- **Version control** — maintain the version history of documents that are revised — drafts of an LPA, successive IC papers — so the current and prior versions are both addressable.
- **Access control and information barriers** — enforce who may see which documents, including the information-barrier restrictions SD-10.4 requires.
- **Retention and disposal enforcement** — apply the retention schedule — hold documents for their required period and dispose of them when it expires — and apply legal holds where they override the schedule.
- **Document-to-record linking** — maintain the link between a document and the durable record it concerns (a holding, a fund, a legal entity, a transaction), so unstructured content is queryable in the correct context.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** documents from across the firm — manager reports and statements (from manager portals and administrators), legal agreements (BD-04, SD-14.9), IC papers (SD-04.5), trade confirmations (SD-12.3), research notes (BD-02), board minutes (SD-16.1); the retention schedule and access policy.
- **Outputs:** the governed document repository and the registered document metadata — consumed by SD-13.6 GP & Manager Report Ingestion (the documents it extracts from), SD-14.9 Legal & Contract Management, SD-03.3 Operational Due Diligence, SD-14.8 Internal Audit, and any domain needing the source document behind a data point.

## Entities

- **Owns:** E-15 Document Metadata — the structured record *about* a source document: what it is, its filing and as-of dates, the durable record it concerns, its storage location and source. SD-13.11 owns the document-as-artefact and its metadata; it does not own the durable records the documents *concern*.
- **Consumes:** the durable records documents are linked to — E-01 Legal Entity, E-02 Instrument / Asset, E-03 Portfolio / Mandate, E-04 Holding / Position (any `book` — documents link to positions on either book), PM-01 Fund & Vehicle, PM-09 Fund Investment — as the link targets; the SD-04.5 documents from across the firm; the SD-12.3 documents from across the firm; the SD-14.9 documents from across the firm; the SD-16.1 documents from across the firm.
- **Note:** E-15 is the provenance and retrieval backbone for the unstructured estate — every figure SD-13.6 extracts is traceable to an E-15 record.

## Standards

- **Records-management and retention** — the document-retention discipline; retention periods are driven by regulatory and legal requirements that vary by jurisdiction and document type.
- **DAMA-DMBOK** — the document- and content-management knowledge area.
- **Information-barrier requirements** — the access-control restrictions are driven by SD-10.4 Restricted & Watch List Management and the firm's information-barrier policy.

## Open extensions

- The relationship between E-15 and the records it links to as a formal model edge.
- The legal-hold model — how a hold overrides the standard retention schedule.
- Content-classification automation — tagging documents by type and subject on ingest.
