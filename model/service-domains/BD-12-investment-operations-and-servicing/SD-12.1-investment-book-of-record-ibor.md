# SD-12.1 — Investment Book of Record (IBOR)

**Business Domain:** BD-12 Investment Operations & Servicing (Back office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Maintains the real-time, intraday view of positions, cash and exposures the front office trades and manages against. The IBOR is one of the two books of record the buy-side keeps: the *current, transaction-aware* statement of what every portfolio holds, updated as orders fill and events occur, available start-of-day and refreshed through the day. It exists so portfolio managers, traders and risk see a position that reflects every committed trade now — not the accounting-basis position struck for the prior close. It is **not** the official accounting book — that is SD-12.2 ABOR — and it is not where the value of a holding is determined — that is SD-08. The IBOR keeps the position; SD-08 prices it; SD-12.2 keeps its accounting-basis twin.

## Service Operations

- **Position keeping** — maintain the current quantity, commitment and value of every holding in every portfolio, as a continuously-updated record on the `ibor` book.
- **Transaction posting** — apply each investment event — trade, subscription, capital call, distribution, corporate action, transfer — to the affected positions as it is captured, so the book reflects committed activity in near real time.
- **Start-of-day position generation** — strike the opening position for every portfolio at the start of the trading day, the baseline the desk and pre-trade compliance work from.
- **Intraday position update** — refresh positions through the day as executions, allocations and cash movements arrive, so the front office sees the effect of its own activity.
- **Cash and exposure projection** — maintain the projected cash balance and exposure for each portfolio, including the cash impact of unsettled trades, so the desk trades against an accurate available-cash figure.
- **Pending-activity tracking** — hold trades that are agreed but not yet settled, and corporate actions and income announced but not yet paid, as projected adjustments to the current position.
- **Position-data distribution** — publish the IBOR position to the front-office, risk and compliance domains that read it as their picture of what the firm owns.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** transactions from SD-06 Trading & Execution (executed and allocated trades), SD-12.8 (capital calls and distributions), SD-12.6 (corporate-action effects); valuations from SD-08; the prior book close from SD-12.2.
- **Outputs:** the real-time IBOR position, cash and exposure record — consumed by SD-05.2 Portfolio Management & Monitoring, SD-06.1 Order Management (pre-trade position check), SD-07 Investment Risk (all domains), SD-10.1 Investment Guideline Monitoring, SD-12.10 Reconciliation (the IBOR side of the reconciliation), and SD-13.10 Investment Reporting & Dashboards.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-07 Valuation (any `method` — IBOR applies whichever valuation method is current for each position), E-08 Price & Market Data, E-03 Portfolio / Mandate; the SD-12.2 prior book close; transactions from SD-06 Trading & Execution (executed and allocated trades), SD-12.8 (capital calls and distributions), SD-12.6 (corporate-action effects).
- **Owns:** **E-04 Holding / Position** on the `book = ibor` partition (co-equal with SD-12.2 on the `book = abor` partition; the key-partitioned pattern is recorded in [`ownership-map.md`](../../ownership-map.md)) — SD-12.1 is the authoritative source for the real-time position on the IBOR book. Also owns **E-05 Transaction** (transactions update the book of record), **E-06 Cash Flow Event** (the granular event and cash records the book is built from), and **PM-09 Fund Investment** — the LP's holding in a fund, which is a Holding / Position specialisation maintained on the IBOR book.
- **Open question:** the IBOR / ABOR reconciliation model — how the two books' positions of E-04 differ and how breaks between them are represented — is flagged on E-04 itself; it bears on both SD-12.1 and SD-12.2.

## Standards

- No single external standard governs the IBOR; it is an industry operating-model concept rather than a published standard. ISO 20022 and ISO 15022 messaging carry the transactions and events the book is updated from.
- The IBOR / ABOR distinction is itself a recognised buy-side architecture pattern — articulated across the major operating-model literature and reflected in the platform vendor stacks (Aladdin, SimCorp, State Street Alpha / Charles River).

## Open extensions

- The Service-Operation-level input/output contracts.
- The IBOR / ABOR reconciliation sub-model — the E-04 open question above.
- The treatment of multi-asset positions — listed instrument, fund interest, OTC derivative, directly-held real asset — on a single book.
