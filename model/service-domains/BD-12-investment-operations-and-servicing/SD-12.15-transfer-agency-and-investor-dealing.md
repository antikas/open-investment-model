# SD-12.15 — Transfer Agency & Investor Dealing

**Business Domain:** BD-12 Investment Operations & Servicing (Back office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Maintains the fund investor register and processes the dealing of fund units — the subscriptions, redemptions and switches of an open-ended fund's investors. SD-12.15 is the transfer-agency capability: it keeps the register of who owns units in each fund, processes investor subscription, redemption and switch orders against the dealing cut-off and the struck NAV, settles the subscription and redemption cash, pays the distributions, and issues the contract notes and statements. Fund administration is fund accounting plus transfer agency; SD-12.15 is the transfer-agency half, the sibling of SD-12.9 Fund Accounting & NAV. It exists because the open-ended fund's investor dealing is a distinct operational discipline with no equivalent elsewhere in BD-12 — SD-12.8 processes the *closed-end* capital-call and distribution events; SD-12.15 processes the *open-ended* registered-fund investor dealing. It is **not** the commercial client relationship — that is BD-15's client-management cluster; SD-12.15 runs the processing engine and the register, BD-15 owns the relationship. As the transfer-agency half of fund administration, it follows the same activation switch as its sibling SD-12.9: an institution that operates no funds of its own — a pure investor that only invests its own or its clients' capital — leaves SD-12.15 dormant.

## Service Operations

- **Maintain the investor register** — keep the register of holders of each fund's units, and the ownership and transfer record.
- **Process subscriptions and redemptions** — process investor subscription, redemption and switch orders against the dealing cut-off and the struck NAV.
- **Settle investor cash** — settle the subscription and redemption cash, and reconcile the dealing cash.
- **Pay distributions** — calculate and pay the income and capital distributions to the fund's investors.
- **Issue contract notes and statements** — issue the deal confirmations, contract notes and periodic investor statements.
- **Apply dealing controls** — apply the anti-dilution-levy / swing-pricing inputs, the large-deal and dilution controls, and the dealing-suspension handling.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** investor dealing orders, from the BD-15 client channels and the platforms; the struck NAV from SD-12.9; the onboarded investors from SD-15.11; the fund terms (PM-10).
- **Outputs:** the updated investor register, the processed deals, the settled investor cash and the contract notes — consumed by SD-12.9 Fund Accounting & NAV (the units in issue), SD-12.1 IBOR and SD-12.2 ABOR (the cash and position effects), SD-12.10 Reconciliation, SD-15.14 Client & Investor Reporting and SD-14.3 Financial Crime Prevention (the monitoring of investor flows).

## Entities

- **Consumes:** PM-01 Fund & Vehicle, PM-10 Fund Terms, E-01 Legal Entity (the investors), E-06 Cash Flow Event; the SD-12.9 struck NAV; the SD-15.11 onboarded investors.
- **Owns:** the fund investor register — currently a process artefact.
- **Open question:** whether an **Investor Register / Unitholding** entity is warranted — the holding of fund units by an investor, the open-ended parallel to PM-06 LP Commitment.

## Standards

- The **UCITS / AIFMD** dealing and investor-register requirements.
- **ISO 20022** investment-fund messaging — the `setr` order and `semt` statement message families.
- The anti-dilution and **swing-pricing** conventions; AML / KYC at the transfer-agency layer (cross-reference SD-14.3).

## Open extensions

- An **Investor Register / Unitholding** entity.
- The swing-pricing and dealing-control sub-model.
- The Service-Operation-level input/output contracts.
