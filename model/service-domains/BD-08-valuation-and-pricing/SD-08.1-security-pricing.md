# SD-08.1 — Security Pricing

**Business Domain:** BD-08 Valuation & Pricing (Middle office) · **Applies:** PUB

## Purpose

Sources, validates and applies a market price for every instrument that has one — whether a firm quoted price from an exchange or a contributed quote, or a vendor evaluated (matrix) price for an instrument that trades but not continuously. SD-08.1 is the capability that answers *what is the market saying this is worth*: it does not itself model a value — that is SD-08.2 — it selects, from the price sources the outside world supplies, the price the firm will use, and proves that price fit to use. The distinction that runs through BD-08: SD-08.1 produces a price the firm **sources and validates**; SD-08.2 produces a value the firm **models itself**. SD-08.1 is **not** raw market-data delivery — that is SD-13.4, which runs the vendor feeds; SD-08.1 takes that data and turns it into the firm's chosen, validated price for a position it actually holds.

## Service Operations

- **Source and select the price** — gather candidate prices from exchanges, contributed-quote feeds and evaluated-pricing vendors, and select the price the firm will use under the pricing hierarchy — the documented order of preference among sources.
- **Apply evaluated and matrix pricing** — for instruments that trade but not continuously, most of the fixed-income universe, take and validate vendor evaluated prices, which interpolate a price from comparable instruments and observable inputs.
- **Run price-tolerance and movement checks** — compare each price against the prior price, against an independent source and against a tolerance band, and flag prices that move more than expected.
- **Detect stale and missing prices** — identify instruments whose price has not moved or has not arrived, and apply the stale-price policy.
- **Apply fair-value adjustments for market timing** — where a market closed before the valuation point — a foreign exchange closed hours earlier — apply the fair-value-adjustment factor so the price reflects the valuation moment, not the close.
- **Maintain the price record** — record the chosen price, its source and its validation status as the validated valuation input the rest of the firm consumes.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** exchange and contributed-quote feeds; vendor evaluated-pricing feeds; the instrument master from SD-13.3; the security-level market data SD-13.4 delivers; the pricing hierarchy and tolerance policy from SD-08.4.
- **Outputs:** validated security prices with source and validation status — recorded as Valuation (E-07), consumed by SD-12.9 Fund Accounting & NAV, BD-07 Investment Risk, BD-09 Performance & Analytics, and SD-08.6, which independently verifies them.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-02 Instrument / Asset, E-08 Price & Market Data, E-03 Portfolio / Mandate; the SD-08.4 pricing hierarchy and tolerance policy; the SD-13.3 instrument master; the security-level market data SD-13.4 delivers.
- **Owns:** the observable-price valuations within E-07 Valuation — E-07 names SD-08.1 as the owner for observable-price valuations. The raw price record itself is E-08 Price & Market Data, owned by SD-13.4; SD-08.1 is its principal validating consumer, not its owner.

## Standards

- **IFRS 13 / ASC 820** fair-value hierarchy — a quoted price in an active market is a Level 1 input; an evaluated price built on observable inputs is Level 2.
- **ISO 20022** and vendor market-data conventions for price delivery.
- **SEC Rule 2a-5** and the **AIFMD** valuation regime treat the selection and validation of prices as part of a documented valuation process.
