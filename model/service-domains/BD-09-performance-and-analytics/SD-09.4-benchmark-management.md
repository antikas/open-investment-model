# SD-09.4 — Benchmark Management

**Business Domain:** BD-09 Performance & Analytics (Middle office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Selects, constructs and maintains the benchmarks portfolios are measured against. A return figure is meaningless without a comparator; Benchmark Management is the capability that decides and maintains the comparator. It is the *judgement and administration* side of benchmarks — which benchmark fits which mandate, how a custom or blended benchmark is built, keeping the assignment current — distinct from SD-13.5 Benchmark & Index Data Management, which holds and maintains the benchmark *as a data asset*.

The investment-performance body of knowledge folds benchmark *analysis* into attribution and appraisal; OpenIM keeps Benchmark Management a Service Domain in its own right because benchmark *administration* — selection, blended construction, assignment, rebalancing governance — is a distinct operational responsibility with its own ownership, separate from the analytics that consume benchmarks.

## Service Operations

- **Benchmark selection** — choose the appropriate benchmark for a portfolio or mandate, given its asset class, strategy and objective.
- **Custom and blended benchmark construction** — build a composite benchmark from weighted constituents where no single index fits the mandate.
- **Policy-benchmark construction** — build and govern the total-fund policy benchmark that expresses the strategic asset allocation; a governance artefact, board-approved, distinct from a general blended benchmark.
- **Benchmark assignment** — attach a benchmark to a portfolio and maintain that assignment as mandates change.
- **Benchmark rebalancing governance** — define the rebalancing rule for a custom benchmark and oversee its application.
- **Benchmark appropriateness review** — periodically test whether an assigned benchmark still fits the portfolio it measures, against the recognised validity criteria (Bailey's SAMURAI tests — specified, appropriate, measurable, unambiguous, reflective of current investment opinion, accountable, investable).

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** portfolio and mandate definitions (E-03); the benchmark and index data asset (E-10, maintained by SD-13.5); the strategic asset allocation (SD-01.4) for the policy benchmark.
- **Outputs:** the benchmark assigned to each portfolio, and the definition of any custom or blended benchmark — consumed by SD-09.1 (benchmark-relative return), SD-09.2 (attribution), SD-09.3 (appraisal), and SD-01 (the policy benchmark).

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-10 Benchmark / Index, E-03 Portfolio / Mandate, E-09 Asset Class; the SD-01.4 strategic asset allocation; the SD-13.5 benchmark and index data asset.
- **Owns:** none directly — the benchmark data asset (E-10) is owned by SD-13.5. SD-09.4 owns the *decision* (which benchmark, the custom-benchmark definition); SD-13.5 owns the *data*. A custom benchmark SD-09.4 constructs becomes an E-10 record that SD-13.5 then maintains.

## Standards

- **GIPS** — GIPS requires a benchmark be defined for each composite and that benchmark choice be disclosed; benchmark appropriateness is a GIPS consideration governed with SD-09.6.
