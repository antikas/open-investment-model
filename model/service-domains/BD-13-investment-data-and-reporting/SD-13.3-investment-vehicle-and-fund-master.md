# SD-13.3 — Investment Vehicle & Fund Master

**Business Domain:** BD-13 Investment Data & Reporting (Cross-cutting — data) · **Applies:** PRIV

## Purpose

Maintains the golden record of investment funds and the legal vehicles that make them up — the reference data for the funds an institution commits to, their GP / management companies, their administrators, and the family hierarchy that ties the related vehicles of a single raise together. A "fund" as an investor experiences it is rarely one legal entity: a single raise is commonly a main fund, parallel vehicles, feeders, co-investment vehicles and continuation vehicles. SD-13.3 is the master that holds both the individual-vehicle record and the fund-family hierarchy. It is the private-markets master that the liquid-markets vendor stacks were never built for. A fund interest *is* an instrument — so SD-13.1 owns the generic instrument shell of a fund interest, and SD-13.3 owns the fund-specific structure, terms and family hierarchy that hangs off it.

## Service Operations

- **Fund onboarding** — create the golden record for a fund being committed to: assign the golden key, capture vintage, strategy, target size, domicile and structure, and link it to its instrument shell (SD-13.1) and its GP and administrator (legal entities owned by SD-13.2).
- **Vehicle-hierarchy maintenance** — record and maintain the multi-vehicle structure of a fund — main fund, parallel vehicles, feeders, co-invest vehicles, continuation vehicles — and the fund-family grouping, so that both "my position in this vehicle" and "my total commitment across this fund family" can be answered.
- **Fund-terms registry** — capture and maintain the standing terms of a fund as reference data: management-fee rate and basis, carried-interest rate and hurdle, the waterfall type, the commitment period, fund life and extension options. The *negotiated, per-LP* side-letter terms are SD-10.5's; SD-13.3 holds the *base* fund terms.
- **GP and management-company reference** — maintain the reference link from a fund to its GP / management company and key persons, drawing the legal entities from SD-13.2 and recording the fund-specific relationship attributes.
- **Administrator and service-provider reference** — record the fund administrator, depositary and other service providers attached to each vehicle, supporting operational due diligence (SD-03.3) and reconciliation (SD-12.10).
- **Fund entity resolution** — when a fund or vehicle arrives from a manager document, a data vendor or a commitment process, decide whether it is an existing record under a variant name or a new vehicle; private funds resolve almost entirely on alias and attribute match, having few standard identifiers.
- **Manager-succession handling** — record the events that change a fund's manager or key-person line-up — GP restructurings, spin-outs, key-person departures — and maintain the reference record through them.
- **Master distribution** — publish the curated fund and vehicle records to the consuming domains as the single authoritative fund reference.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the fund-commitment process (SD-03.5) and its legal documents — the LPA and subscription documents, parsed by SD-13.6 and SD-13.11; manager and administrator data; the legal entities for GPs and administrators from SD-13.2.
- **Outputs:** the golden fund and vehicle record — consumed by BD-03 Manager & Fund Investment, SD-01.10 Commitment Pacing, SD-08.3 Private-Asset Valuation, SD-09.7 / SD-09.8 private-markets analytics, SD-11.6 Fund Finance & Capital-Call Liquidity, SD-12.8 Capital Call & Distribution Processing, SD-12.9 Fund Accounting, and SD-13.10 reporting.

## Entities

- **Owns:** PM-01 Fund & Vehicle (the golden fund record, including the Fund Family / Group grouping); PM-02 GP / Management Company *as a fund reference relationship* (the underlying legal entity is owned by SD-13.2); PM-03 Fund Administrator (likewise); PM-05 Legal Vehicle; PM-10 Fund Terms (the base, non-side-letter terms); PM-11 Manager Succession Event; the fund-side rows of E-13 Entity Alias and E-14 External Identifier.
- **Consumes:** E-01 Legal Entity (the GP, management-company and administrator parties, from SD-13.2); E-02 Instrument / Asset (the fund-interest instrument shell, from SD-13.1); E-09 Asset Class; the SD-03.5 fund-commitment process; the SD-13.6 parsed LPA and subscription documents; the SD-13.11 parsed LPA and subscription documents.
- **Open question:** the split between PM-02 / PM-03 as *fund-relationship* records here and the underlying E-01 legal entity in SD-13.2 — confirm the ownership boundary. The in-record-array versus normalised E-13 / E-14 question is shared with SD-13.1 and SD-13.2.

## Standards

- **FIBO** — fund and collective-investment-vehicle concepts align to FIBO Funds semantics where they are modelled there.
- **ILPA** (Institutional Limited Partners Association) — the **ILPA Reporting Template**, **ILPA Capital Call & Distribution Template** and the **ILPA Model Limited Partnership Agreement** (with **ILPA Principles 3.0**) shape the fund-terms registry vocabulary; the negotiated side-letter layer is SD-10.5's.
- **Identifier reality** — private funds have few standard identifiers; some carry an LEI for the legal vehicle. The master is built for the no-universal-identifier case and resolves on aliases.
