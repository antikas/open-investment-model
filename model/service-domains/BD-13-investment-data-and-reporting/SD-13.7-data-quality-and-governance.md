# SD-13.7 — Data Quality & Governance

**Business Domain:** BD-13 Investment Data & Reporting (Cross-cutting — data) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Defines and enforces data quality, ownership and stewardship across the institution's investment data. Where the other BD-13 Service Domains *produce* data — masters, feeds, extractions, metrics — SD-13.7 is the capability that holds them all to a standard: it sets the quality rules, names the data owners and stewards, runs the workflows that detect and remediate issues, and maintains the lineage that makes a data point traceable to its source. It is a *governance* Service Domain — it does not source, curate or interpret data itself; it governs how the domains that do are run. It is the BD-13 analogue of an investment-compliance function: a control layer over the data estate.

## Service Operations

- **Data-quality rule definition** — define the quality rules that data must satisfy — completeness, validity, consistency, timeliness, uniqueness, accuracy — per data domain, and the tolerance and severity of each.
- **Data-quality monitoring** — run the defined rules continuously across the masters, feeds and certified datasets, and produce data-quality scores and dashboards.
- **Data ownership and stewardship assignment** — name the accountable data owner and the operational data steward for each data domain, and maintain the stewardship operating model.
- **Issue detection and remediation workflow** — raise a data-quality issue when a rule fails, route it to the responsible steward, track it to resolution, and analyse recurring issues for root cause.
- **Data-lineage tracking** — maintain the lineage of each significant data element — where it originated, what transformations it passed through, which datasets and reports it flows into — so a figure can be traced end to end.
- **Data-policy and standards governance** — own the firm's data policies (classification, retention, access, quality standards) and the change process for them.
- **Data-governance forum operation** — operate the data-governance committee or forum where ownership disputes, policy changes and material data-quality issues are adjudicated.
- **Data-quality reporting** — report the state of data quality and the open-issue backlog to data owners and to enterprise governance (BD-14).

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the data produced across BD-13 (and the operational data of BD-01 to BD-12) as the population the quality rules run over; the firm's data policies; the vendor-quality signals from SD-13.4.
- **Outputs:** data-quality scores and dashboards; the stewardship assignments and the issue backlog; data lineage; the governed data policies — consumed by every BD-13 Service Domain (as the standard they are held to), by SD-13.8 (only quality-passing data is certified), by SD-13.10 reporting, and by SD-14.8 Internal Audit and SD-14.7 Internal Control & Assurance.

## Entities

- **Owns:** **E-11 Classification Type & Value** (the extensible taxonomy behind every time-varying classification — sector, geography, ESG strategy, fund stage) and **E-12 Classification History** (the bi-temporal record of which value applied to which subject across which interval) — the *governance entities*. SD-13.7 also owns the data-quality rule set, the stewardship model, the issue backlog and the lineage records as Service-Domain artefacts (an open question is whether these should themselves be first-class entities — see below). SD-13.7 does not own a business-data master; it owns the classification machinery and the metadata that governs the masters.
- **Consumes:** every master and dataset across BD-13 as the population it monitors — E-01, E-02, E-07 (any `method`), E-08, E-10, PM-01, and the rest. *(SD-13.7's consumption of its own owned E-11 / E-12 is the consumption-of-owned-entity case; consumers of E-11 / E-12 across the model — the issuer-sector, instrument-classification, geography classifications — read against SD-13.7's authoritative source.)*; the SD-13.4 vendor-quality signals.
- **Open question:** whether the data-quality rule, the data-quality issue and the lineage record should be modelled as first-class entities — a governance-metadata extension to the entity model. They are currently the Service Domain's internal artefacts.

## Standards

- **DAMA-DMBOK** (Data Management Body of Knowledge) — the reference framework for the data-management knowledge areas, including the data-quality dimensions SD-13.7 measures against.
- **EDM Council DCAM** (Data Management Capability Assessment Model) — the capability-maturity framework used to assess and benchmark the data-governance and data-quality functions; an EDM Council standard widely adopted across the buy-side.
- **ISO 8000** — the data-quality standard, where the firm formalises data-quality requirements.

## Open extensions

- The governance-metadata entities question — data-quality rule, issue, lineage record as first-class entities.
- The DCAM capability-maturity assessment as a recurring, scored operation.
- The interaction with SD-14.4 Model Governance where a model consumes data the quality of which SD-13.7 governs.
