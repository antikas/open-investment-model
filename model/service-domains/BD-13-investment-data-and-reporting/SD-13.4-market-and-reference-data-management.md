# SD-13.4 — Market & Reference Data Management

**Business Domain:** BD-13 Investment Data & Reporting (Cross-cutting — data) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Sources, validates and distributes the market and reference data the institution runs on — prices, yields, rates, FX, credit ratings, corporate-action notifications, instrument-reference and entity-reference feeds. Market & Reference Data Management is the *data-supply pipe*: it manages the relationships with data vendors, brings their feeds in, normalises them to one internal shape, applies first-line validation, and distributes the result to the domains that consume it. It is distinct from the master Service Domains it *feeds* — SD-13.1, SD-13.2 and SD-13.3 curate the slow-changing golden records, and SD-13.4 sources the vendor feeds those masters are populated from, as well as the fast-moving market data that is not master data at all and that valuation and risk consume directly.

## Service Operations

- **Vendor-feed management** — manage the contracts, entitlements and connections to data vendors (the major market-data and reference-data providers and the exchanges), and the licensing terms that constrain how their data may be used and redistributed.
- **Feed ingestion and scheduling** — bring in the scheduled feeds — start-of-day, intraday, end-of-day, corporate-action — and monitor for late, incomplete or failed deliveries.
- **Data normalisation** — map each vendor's format, codes and conventions onto OpenIM's internal shape, so a consuming domain reads one consistent structure regardless of source.
- **First-line data validation** — apply mechanical checks on arrival: tolerance and staleness checks on prices and rates, completeness checks on reference feeds, cross-vendor comparison where more than one source covers the same instrument.
- **Source hierarchy and golden-source selection** — where several vendors supply the same data point, apply the configured source-of-record hierarchy to choose the value that is distributed.
- **Reference-data distribution** — publish validated market and reference data to the consuming domains and to the data platform, on the schedule each consumer needs.
- **Vendor-quality monitoring** — track each feed's timeliness, completeness and accuracy over time, and feed the result into vendor reviews (SD-17.8) and the data-quality function (SD-13.7).

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** vendor and exchange feeds — prices, rates, FX, ratings, corporate-action notifications, instrument- and entity-reference data; the source hierarchy and validation rules.
- **Outputs:** normalised, validated market and reference data — consumed by SD-08.1 Security Pricing and the rest of BD-08 Valuation & Pricing, BD-07 Investment Risk, SD-13.1 / SD-13.2 (reference feeds that populate the masters), SD-13.5 (the index-provider universes), and the data platform (SD-13.12). Corporate-action notifications feed SD-12.6.

## Entities

- **Owns:** **E-08 Price & Market Data** (observed prices, yields, rates, FX — the fast-moving observable inputs) and **E-09 Asset Class** (the asset-class taxonomy across public and private markets, applied to E-02 instruments and PM/PB/DR/RA specialisations)
- **Consumes:** E-02 Instrument / Asset and E-01 Legal Entity (to attach incoming data to the right golden record); E-14 External Identifier (any partition — instrument-side, entity-side and fund-side identifiers are all used to join vendor data on external identifiers).
- **Note:** E-08 is observed market data, not master data — it is high-volume and time-series, and SD-13.4 owns it precisely because it is feed-sourced rather than curated. The instrument and entity *masters* the feeds populate are owned by SD-13.1 and SD-13.2.

## Standards

- **Identifier standards** — ISIN, CUSIP, SEDOL, FIGI, LEI — used to join vendor feeds onto the internal golden keys via E-14.
- **Market-data licensing** — vendor and exchange licensing terms are a hard operational constraint on redistribution; managed as part of vendor-feed management.
- **DAMA-DMBOK** and the **EDM Council DCAM** framework — the data-management discipline; market and reference data quality is a DCAM-assessed capability.

## Open extensions

- The source-hierarchy and golden-source-selection logic as a configurable rule set.
- The split of corporate-action *notification* (sourced here) from corporate-action *processing* (SD-12.6).
- Real-time / streaming market data versus batch reference data as distinct ingestion patterns.
