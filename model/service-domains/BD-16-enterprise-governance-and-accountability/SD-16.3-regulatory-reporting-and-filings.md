# SD-16.3 — Regulatory Reporting & Filings

**Business Domain:** BD-16 Enterprise Governance & Accountability (Cross-cutting — corporate) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Produces and submits the firm's mandatory regulatory returns and filings, and runs the regulator relationship. SD-16.3 is the capability that meets the firm's obligation, as a licensed entity, to report to its regulators — the corporate and prudential returns (capital adequacy, the firm's own conduct returns) and the investment and fund regulatory returns (Form PF, AIFMD Annex IV, MiFIR transaction reporting, Form 13F, short-selling disclosures). It owns the *filing* — the return's preparation, submission and the regulator liaison; the *data* the return is built from comes from BD-12 and BD-13. It is **not** investment-compliance monitoring — that is BD-10, which checks the portfolio against the rules — and it is not the firm's statutory financial statements, which are SD-16.4.

## Service Operations

- **Prepare the corporate and prudential returns** — the firm's own regulatory returns as a licensed entity: capital-adequacy reporting, the prudential return, the firm's conduct returns.
- **Prepare the investment and fund regulatory returns** — Form PF, AIFMD Annex IV, MiFIR transaction reporting, Form 13F and the short-selling and position disclosures, drawing the data from BD-12 and BD-13.
- **Submit and track filings** — submit each return on its deadline through the regulator's channel, and track the filing calendar and confirmations.
- **Run the regulator relationship** — manage the operational interaction with the firm's regulators: requests, queries, examinations and notifications.
- **Maintain the regulatory-filing inventory** — the register of every return the firm owes, its frequency, its owner and its status.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the corporate and capital data from SD-17.1; portfolio, transaction and fund data from BD-12 and BD-13; breach-disclosure triggers from SD-10.8; the regulatory-filing requirements.
- **Outputs:** the submitted regulatory returns and the regulator-relationship record — consumed by the regulators, the governing bodies, and SD-14.8 Internal Audit.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-14 External Identifier (any partition — instrument-side LEIs / ISINs, entity-side LEIs, fund-side identifiers are all carried in regulatory returns depending on the regime) and the portfolio / transaction entities the returns draw on; E-01 Legal Entity (the regulated entities and the regulators); the SD-10.8 breach-disclosure triggers; the SD-17.1 corporate and capital data; **E-35 Complaint Record** (from SD-15.16 — the complaint statistics that feed the regulatory complaints return the FCA DISP and equivalent regimes require).
- **Owns:** the regulatory-filing inventory and submission record — a process artefact; a Regulatory Filing entity is an open extension.

## Standards

- The prudential regimes — the UK **IFPR**, the EU **IFR/IFD**, and equivalent capital regimes for investment firms.
- The investment / fund reporting regimes — **Form PF**, **AIFMD Annex IV**, **MiFIR** transaction reporting, **Form 13F**, the short-selling disclosure regimes.
- The regulators' filing channels and data standards.

## Open extensions

- A Regulatory Filing entity.
- The corporate / prudential versus investment / fund return sub-model.
- The BD-12 / BD-13 data-sourcing interface.
