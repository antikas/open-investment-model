# SD-09.5 — Investment Analytics & Insight

**Business Domain:** BD-09 Performance & Analytics (Middle office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Produces the forward-looking and diagnostic analytics that inform investment decisions — factor and exposure analytics, what-if analysis, portfolio diagnostics, peer comparison. Where SD-09.1, SD-09.2 and SD-09.3 are the *ex-post* account of results (what return, from where, was it worth the risk), Investment Analytics & Insight is the *ex-ante and diagnostic* service: the analysis the front office uses to decide what to do next. It is the analytical workbench of the middle office.

The boundary is deliberate: ex-post realised risk-adjusted performance is SD-09.3 Performance Appraisal, not here; ESG and climate *data* is SD-13.9 ESG & Sustainability Data and climate-risk *analytics* is SD-07.8 Climate Risk Analytics — SD-09.5 *consumes* those as inputs to portfolio diagnostics but does not produce them.

## Service Operations

- **Factor and exposure analytics** — decompose a portfolio's risk and return into systematic factor exposures; the factor model other domains (SD-09.2 attribution, SD-09.3 appraisal) consume.
- **What-if analysis** — model the effect of a hypothetical, deliberate portfolio change before it is made (distinct from BD-07's stress testing, which models adverse market scenarios, not deliberate portfolio changes).
- **Portfolio diagnostics** — characterise a portfolio: concentration, active share, factor tilts, style, liquidity profile.
- **Ex-ante return and risk analytics** — forward-looking expected-return and tracking-error analysis.
- **Goals-based forward-looking measurement** — for a wealth manager running goals-based allocation (SD-01.13), compute the **probability of meeting each goal** as a forward-looking measure on the per-goal sub-portfolio, consuming the goal hierarchy and the per-goal target allocation SD-01.13 produces. The probability-of-meeting-goal measure sits alongside the composite return on the wealth-manager performance row in the BD-09 README.
- **Peer and universe comparison** — position a portfolio or fund against a peer universe.
- **Interactive and self-service analytics** — the analytical workbench for one-off and exploratory questions across the investment data estate.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** holdings and valuations; market and reference data; benchmark data; classifications; ESG data (SD-13.9) and climate-risk analytics (SD-07.8); the return, attribution and appraisal outputs of SD-09.1 / SD-09.2 / SD-09.3; the **goal hierarchy and per-goal target allocation from SD-01.13** (the inputs to the probability-of-meeting-goal computation).
- **Outputs:** analytics and diagnostics consumed by the front-office decision domains (BD-01 allocation, BD-04 deal screening, BD-05 portfolio management), by SD-09.2 and SD-09.3 (the factor model), and by reporting (SD-13.10); the **probability-of-meeting-goal forward-looking measure**, consumed by SD-01.13 (re-allocation across and within goals) and by SD-15.14 / SD-15.15 (client reporting and the planning review).

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-04 Holding / Position (`book = abor` for realised-return diagnostics, `book = ibor` for live-positioning diagnostics — analytics cuts across both), E-07 Valuation (any `method`), E-08 Price & Market Data, E-10 Benchmark / Index, E-09 Asset Class, E-11 Classification Type & Value, E-12 Classification History; the SD-07.8 climate-risk analytics; the return, attribution and appraisal outputs of SD-09.1 / SD-09.2 / SD-09.3; the SD-13.9 ESG data; **E-30 Goal** (the goal the probability is computed for, framed by SD-01.14) and the **SD-01.13 goal hierarchy and per-goal target allocation**.
- **Owns:** **E-31 Goal Progress Measurement** — the stored, append-only, point-in-time probability of meeting a goal, with the capital-market-assumption set and methodology version behind it (a computed-metric-as-entity). Its other analytics outputs remain transient analytical artefacts, not master entities.

## Standards

- No single external standard governs general investment analytics; where an analytic is presented as performance it conforms to GIPS via SD-09.6.
- The **goals-based-investing body of knowledge** grounds the probability-of-meeting-goal forward-looking measure — **Jean L. P. Brunel**, *Goals-Based Wealth Management: An Integrated and Practical Approach to Changing the Structure of Wealth Advisory Practices* (Wiley, 2015), and **Ashvin B. Chhabra**, *The Aspirational Investor* (HarperBusiness, 2015) and the underlying "Beyond Markowitz" framing of the personal-risk / market-risk / aspirational-risk goals tri-bucket. These ground the per-goal probability measure SD-09.5 computes; the methodology version is held on E-22 Metric Definition.

## Open extensions

- The boundary with BD-07 Investment Risk made fully explicit (deliberate-change what-if here; adverse-scenario stress in SD-07.6).
