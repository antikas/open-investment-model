# SD-02.8 — Research Procurement & Evaluation

**Business Domain:** BD-02 Securities Research & Selection (Front office) · **Applies:** PUB

## Purpose

Sources, budgets, pays for and evaluates the external research the firm consumes — sell-side, independent and expert-network research. Where SD-02.1 to SD-02.7 produce the firm's own research, this Service Domain governs the research it buys. Since MiFID II unbundled research from execution, external research is explicitly priced, budgeted and paid for through a structured evaluation — making research procurement a discrete, regulated capability. It is near-universal: even firms with deep internal research consume external research, and a wealth manager running on curated external research depends on this Service Domain more than on any other in BD-02.

## Service Operations

- **Set the research budget** — determine the annual external-research spend and the research charge, and its basis in the investment-management agreement.
- **Source external research providers** — identify and onboard sell-side, independent and expert-network research providers.
- **Operate the broker vote** — run the periodic structured evaluation in which portfolio managers and analysts score and rank research providers.
- **Allocate research payments** — translate the evaluation into provider payments and the commission allocation across the broker panel.
- **Administer the Research Payment Account** — manage the client-funded or profit-and-loss-funded account research is paid from.
- **Evidence unbundling compliance** — maintain the records demonstrating research is separately priced, budgeted and valued per the MiFID II regime.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** external research output; portfolio-manager and analyst evaluation scores; the research budget; trading-commission data.
- **Outputs:** the provider ranking, the research-payment allocation, the budget-versus-spend record and the compliance evidence — consumed by **BD-06 Trading & Execution** (through which the commission allocations settle), by **BD-10 Investment Compliance** (the MiFID II research-unbundling rule's pre-trade obligations), by **BD-14 SD-14.2 Corporate Compliance & Conduct** (the conduct-compliance assessment of the firm's research arrangements), and by **BD-16** for governance reporting.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** Legal Entity (E-01) — research providers as a role of the party master.
- **Owns:** none — the provider evaluation and the payment allocation are operating-model artefacts.

## Standards

- **MiFID II** research-unbundling rules — the separation of research from execution, the research budget and research charge, and the Research Payment Account. The **broker vote** (also provider or broker evaluation) is the industry-standard provider-evaluation methodology, which the regime requires to combine a defensible quantitative and qualitative basis.

## Open extensions

- The boundary with BD-06 Trading & Execution on commission settlement — SD-02.8 allocates; BD-06 settles.
- Whether the external-research-provider evaluation warrants a record structure, parallel to a service-provider register.
- Jurisdictional variation — the unbundling regime differs across markets; the Service Domain is written to the MiFID II form as the most demanding case.
