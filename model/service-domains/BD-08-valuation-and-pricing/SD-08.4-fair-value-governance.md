# SD-08.4 — Fair-Value Governance

**Business Domain:** BD-08 Valuation & Pricing (Middle office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Governs how the firm values everything it holds — the valuation policy, the pricing hierarchy, the methodologies, and the committee that adjudicates the hard cases. SD-08.4 owns the **rules** of valuation, not the production of any one mark. It exists because a valuation is only as trustworthy as the governance around it: every other BD-08 Service Domain operates inside the policy SD-08.4 sets, and a contested or hard-to-value mark is escalated to its pricing committee. This is the Service Domain that is **never delegable** — a firm may outsource valuation *production* to a fund administrator, but the valuation *policy and oversight* remain its own responsibility (SEC Rule 2a-5's "valuation designee" oversight, AIFMD Article 19). SD-08.4 is not valuation model governance — the enterprise model inventory and independent validation are SD-14.4's; SD-08.4 governs the valuation *policy and outputs*, not the model lifecycle.

## Service Operations

- **Maintain the valuation policy** — own and version the firm's valuation policy: the methodologies, the pricing hierarchy, the source-waterfall, the tolerance thresholds and the stale-price rules.
- **Operate the pricing / valuation committee** — convene the committee that reviews and approves valuations, adjudicates contested marks and signs off the periodic valuation.
- **Classify the fair-value hierarchy level** — determine and govern the Level 1 / 2 / 3 classification of each instrument under IFRS 13 / ASC 820, and the movement of instruments between levels.
- **Oversee pricing vendors and third-party valuers** — approve, monitor and periodically review the pricing-data vendors, evaluated-pricing providers and external valuers the firm relies on.
- **Oversee a delegated valuation provider** — where valuation production is delegated to a fund administrator or valuation agent, exercise the firm's retained oversight of that provider.
- **Govern fair-value disclosure** — own the valuation inputs to the fair-value disclosures in the financial statements and the fair-value-hierarchy reporting.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the valuations produced by SD-08.1, SD-08.2 and SD-08.3; price-challenge and IPV findings from SD-08.6; auditor and regulator feedback; pricing-vendor performance evidence.
- **Outputs:** the valuation policy, the approved valuations, the fair-value-level classifications and the committee minutes — consumed by every BD-08 Service Domain, SD-12.9 Fund Accounting & NAV, the BD-13 reporting domains, SD-14.8 Internal Audit and the external auditor.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-07 Valuation (any `method` — governance runs across the observable-price, mark-to-model and manager-mark / appraisal valuations) and E-02 Instrument / Asset; the valuations produced by SD-08.1, SD-08.2 and SD-08.3; the SD-08.6 price-challenge and IPV findings.
- **Owns:** none as a master entity — SD-08.4 governs E-07 Valuation (E-07 names SD-08.4 as its governing Service Domain) through the valuation policy and the fair-value-level classification. The policy itself is currently a process artefact.
- **Open question:** whether a **Valuation Policy** entity is warranted — a versioned, owned policy record carrying the methodologies, the pricing hierarchy and the tolerance thresholds, parallel in shape to other governance-artefact entities.

## Standards

- **IFRS 13 / ASC 820** fair-value measurement and the three-level hierarchy.
- **SEC Rule 2a-5** — the valuation designee and the good-faith fair-value determination — and **AIFMD Article 19** — the valuation function and its independence.
- **IPEV** and **IVS** as the methodology standards the policy adopts.
- **COSO** and the internal-control frameworks — the pricing committee is a key control, evidenced to SD-14.7 Internal Control & Assurance.

## Open extensions

- The **Valuation Policy** entity — the open question above.
- The fair-value-level (1 / 2 / 3) classification sub-model and the level-transfer rules.
- The pricing-committee operating model — membership, cadence and escalation thresholds.
