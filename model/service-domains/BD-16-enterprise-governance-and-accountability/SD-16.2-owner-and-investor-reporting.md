# SD-16.2 — Owner & Investor Reporting

**Business Domain:** BD-16 Enterprise Governance & Accountability (Cross-cutting — corporate) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Produces the periodic accountability report to whoever the firm answers to — its owners, its beneficiaries, the shareholders of the funds it runs, or its limited partners. SD-16.2 is one capability with many audiences: a sovereign-wealth fund reports to its government, a pension scheme to its trustees and members, a third-party manager to fund boards and shareholders, a private-markets GP to its LPs. The capability is the same — gather the performance, stewardship and accountability data, format it to the audience's standard, distribute it, and handle the queries — only the report template differs. It is **not** the *internal* investment reporting the firm uses to look at itself (that is SD-13.10), and it is **not** the commercial investor-relations and fundraising function — winning investors, the data room, the sales relationship — which belongs to BD-15 Distribution, Product & Client Management (SD-15.13 / SD-15.14). SD-16.2 is *accountability* reporting; it answers to those the firm is accountable to.

## Service Operations

- **Produce the owner / beneficiary report** — the periodic accountability report to a government owner, a board, or a scheme's trustees and members.
- **Produce the investor / LP report** — the capital-account statement, the fund-shareholder report and the periodic LP report, to the relevant disclosure standard.
- **Handle accountability queries** — receive and answer the questions owners, beneficiaries and investors raise on the report.
- **Run the owner / investor meeting** — the mechanics of the annual general meeting, the LP meeting and the owner accountability session.
- **Govern the reporting package** — control the content, accuracy and assurance of the accountability report before it is issued.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** internal investment reporting data and dashboards from SD-13.10; the governed metric definitions from SD-13.8; performance from BD-09; the funds' NAV and capital accounts from SD-12.9; stewardship and voting records from SD-12.12; the GIPS-compliant presentations from SD-09.6.
- **Outputs:** the owner, beneficiary, fund-shareholder and LP accountability reports — consumed by the firm's owners, beneficiaries and investors, and the governing bodies.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** the performance, NAV and stewardship outputs across the model; E-01 Legal Entity (the owners and investors); FO-01 Fund Product (the issued-fund product the accountability reports cover — fund legal structure, regulatory wrapper and governance state from FO-01 feed the fund-shareholder and LP report headers); FO-02 Share / Unit Class (the class the regulatory disclosures and accountability reports cover — class ISIN, distribution policy and `class_fee_schedule` on FO-02 are class-grain figures required for UCITS KIID / PRIIPs KID, fund factsheets and the shareholder report); **FO-03 Investor Unitholding** (the per-investor, per-class unit balance at the register grain — the unitholder register that regulatory and shareholder reporting is produced from, including the register of unitholders entitled to distribution at the record date and the investor-level accountability statements); **FO-06 Fee Accrual** (the `net_charge` by `fee_type` and the OCF/TER reconstruction chain — the UCITS KIID / PRIIPs KID ongoing-charges disclosure, fund factsheet fee tables, and the ILPA fee-and-expense line items in LP reports all source from the stored FO-06 figures rather than from a live recomputation; the disclosed charge is the rendering of FO-06 `net_charge` aggregated over the period and divided by average NAV); the SD-09.6 GIPS-compliant presentations; the SD-12.12 stewardship and voting records; the SD-12.9 funds' NAV and capital accounts; the SD-13.10 internal investment reporting data and dashboards; the SD-13.8 governed metric definitions.
- **Owns:** the accountability-reporting package — a process artefact.

## Standards

- The **ILPA Reporting Template** (v2.0, January 2025) for LP reporting — its Capital Account Statement section carries the capital-account reporting.
- **GIPS** for the performance presentation (the verified methodology is SD-09.6's).
- The **Santiago Principles** for sovereign-wealth-fund owner reporting; fund-shareholder disclosure regulation (the UCITS KIID / PRIIPs KID, fund factsheets).

## Design notes

### Investor and owner accountability report as a derived rendering

The periodic accountability report to the fund's shareholders, unitholders, beneficiaries, LP investors and owners is a **rendering of figures of record already in the model**. It is not a stored entity in its own right. The derivation for the investor-facing periodic report is:

- **Unit balance and cost basis** — FO-03 Investor Unitholding (the register position at the start and end of the reporting period).
- **Dealing events** — FO-04 Dealing Order records (subscriptions, redemptions, transfers) settled during the period.
- **Income distributions** — FO-05 Fund Distribution Event records (declarations, per-unit amounts, pay-date events).
- **NAV-per-unit series** — class-grain E-07 Valuation records (produced by SD-12.9 at each pricing point; these are the struck figures of record).
- **Fee charges and OCF/TER** — FO-06 Fee Accrual (`net_charge` by `fee_type`; the UCITS KIID / PRIIPs KID ongoing-charges disclosure sources from the stored FO-06 figures, not from a live recomputation).
- **Capital account** — PM-13 Investor Capital Account (the periodic roll-forward of contributions, distributions, allocated gain for the closed-end LP case).
- **Performance** — E-20 Performance Result records; the GIPS-verified presentation from SD-09.6.
- **Stewardship** — voting and engagement records from SD-12.12.

No new entity is needed for the report package. The liability attaches to the correctness of the figures, not to the report format. SD-16.2 assembles these figures into the accountability report and governs its accuracy and distribution. The rendering is a process artefact; the figures are the records of truth. This is the same principle as the KIID/KID design note in SD-12.9: the document is the rendering of figures of record, never a separate stored source.

### Guideline compliance vs investor reporting — two different accountabilities

SD-16.2 is the accountability-reporting spine; the mandate-compliance spine is distinct:

- **Mandate and guideline compliance** (the SD-10.x spine — nine Service Domains) answers: *did the portfolio stay within the rules it must obey?* The breach record is E-18 Limit Breach, owned by SD-10.8. The liability is internal and regulatory. The reporting is to the firm's compliance function and the regulator.
- **Owner and investor reporting** (SD-16.2, and SD-15.14 for the commercial-discretionary variant) answers: *what did we tell the investor or owner about performance, costs and holdings, and was it correct?* The liability is to the investor or owner. The records backing the answer are the deterministic-spine figures (E-07, FO-06, FO-03, FO-04, FO-05, E-20, PM-13).

The two accountabilities share the portfolio universe but answer to different principals, carry different liabilities, and are closed through different remediation paths. SD-16.2 owns the investor / owner accountability; SD-10.x owns the mandate-compliance accountability. The boundary is architecturally load-bearing and must not be conflated.
