# SD-05.5 — Cash Equitisation & Drag Management

**Business Domain:** BD-05 Portfolio Management (Front office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Keeps a portfolio's uninvested cash exposed to markets and minimises cash drag. A portfolio always carries operational cash — pending allocations, redemptions, dividend and coupon receipts, rebalancing cash — and uninvested cash is a drag on return whenever markets rise. Cash Equitisation overlays index futures or ETFs on that cash so it tracks the target exposure. It is a purpose-specific overlay — it shares the futures and collateral machinery of SD-05.4 but its purpose is drag elimination, not exposure shaping.

## Service Operations

- **Identify and monitor operational cash** — track the unintended and operational cash balances across the portfolio.
- **Measure cash drag** — quantify the basis-point cost of idle cash against the benchmark.
- **Equitise cash** — overlay index futures or ETFs so that cash plus the synthetic position tracks the target market exposure.
- **Size and roll the synthetic exposure** — size the contracts to the cash balance, roll the futures, and manage the margin.
- **Attribute the drag outcome** — report the drag avoided and the residual.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the portfolio's cash balances and cash forecasts; the target beta or duration; futures and ETF market data.
- **Outputs:** the equitisation positions and the cash-drag attribution.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** Portfolio / Mandate (E-03), Holding / Position (E-04, `book = ibor`), Price & Market Data (E-08).
- **Owns:** none — the equitisation positions are recorded as holdings (E-04).

## Standards

- No external standard governs cash equitisation. It is an established practitioner technique, executed with equity-index futures.

## Open extensions

- The shared machinery with SD-05.4 — the split is by purpose (drag elimination versus exposure shaping), not mechanism.
- The interaction with SD-05.6 on cash held deliberately for liquidity rather than operationally.
