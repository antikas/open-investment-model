# SD-13.8 — Semantic & Metric Layer

**Business Domain:** BD-13 Investment Data & Reporting (Cross-cutting — data) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Maintains the governed definitions of metrics and the semantic layer over the institution's investment data — the single, agreed answer to "what does this number mean and how is it calculated." A figure such as time-weighted return, TVPI, active risk, exposure or AUM is only comparable across the firm if it is defined once; SD-13.8 is the Service Domain that holds that definition. It is the *definitional* layer: it owns the metric definition, the calculation logic and the certified datasets, but it does *not* compute the metric in production — BD-09 computes performance, BD-07 computes risk, BD-12 computes accounting figures, each to the definition SD-13.8 governs. One metric, defined once here, computed by whichever domain owns the result.

## Service Operations

- **Metric definition** — define each governed metric: its precise meaning, its formula, its inputs, its currency and period conventions, the asset classes it applies to, and any variant forms (gross / net, before / after fees).
- **Calculation-logic governance** — own the documented calculation logic for each metric and the change-control process for it, so a methodology change is deliberate, versioned and propagated.
- **Semantic-layer maintenance** — maintain the business-friendly semantic model over the physical data — the dimensions, the conformed entities, the relationships — that lets reporting and analytics be expressed in business terms rather than physical table structure.
- **Certified-dataset publication** — publish the curated, quality-passed, definition-conformant datasets that the firm's reporting and analytics are expected to draw on, and mark them as certified.
- **Metric catalogue and glossary** — maintain the catalogue of governed metrics and the business glossary, so any consumer can discover the agreed definition and its owner.
- **Metric-lineage and dependency management** — record which metrics depend on which inputs and on which other metrics, so the effect of a definition change can be traced.
- **Definition-conflict resolution** — adjudicate where two parts of the firm use the same term for different things (or different terms for the same thing) and converge them on one governed definition.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the calculation methodologies governed by the computing domains — the SD-09.6 GIPS-governed performance methodology, the risk methodologies of BD-07, the accounting bases of BD-12; the quality-passed data from SD-13.7; the physical data estate.
- **Outputs:** the governed metric definitions, the semantic model and the certified datasets — consumed by SD-13.10 Investment Reporting & Dashboards (which reports to these definitions), by BD-09, BD-07 and BD-12 (which compute *to* these definitions), and by BD-16's external-reporting Service Domains (which present figures defined here).

## Entities

- **Owns:** **E-22 Metric Definition** — the governed, versioned definition of a metric (formula, inputs, conventions, variant forms) that stored results reference; plus the semantic model and the certified-dataset register as the broader *definitional metadata* over the estate. The Metric Definition is the one governance-layer promotion to a first-class entity, because a stored result (E-20 Performance Result, E-19 Risk Measurement) needs to reference it; the data-quality rule, lineage record and certified-dataset register stay Service-Domain artefacts.
- **Consumes:** the full entity model as the physical substrate the semantic layer is built over — E-04 (any `book` — metric definitions cover both IBOR-basis and ABOR-basis cuts), E-06, E-07 (any `method`), E-08, E-10 and the rest; the SD-09.6 calculation methodologies governed by the computing domains; the SD-13.7 quality-passed data.
- **Note:** performance, risk and accounting figures are **metrics, not entities** — OpenIM models the metric *definition* here in the semantic layer; the stored point-in-time artefacts are E-07 Valuation and E-19 Risk Measurement. BD-09 consumes entities and produces metrics; SD-13.8 is where the metric definition lives.

## Standards

- **GIPS** — the performance-metric definitions SD-13.8 governs must conform to GIPS where the firm claims compliance; the methodology is governed jointly with SD-09.6.
- **ILPA** — the private-markets metric definitions (TVPI, DPI, RVPI, PIC) follow ILPA conventions.
- **DAMA-DMBOK** — metadata management and the business-glossary discipline.
- The semantic-layer concept is implementation-tooling-neutral in the model; OpenIM specifies the governed definition, not the BI or semantic-layer product that hosts it.

## Open extensions

- The metric-definition-as-entity question.
- The certified-dataset register and the certification workflow to full depth.
- The relationship to a stored Performance Result entity (the open SD-09.1 question) — a stored result should reference the metric definition it was computed to.
