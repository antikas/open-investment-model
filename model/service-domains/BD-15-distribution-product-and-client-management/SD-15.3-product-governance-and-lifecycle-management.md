# SD-15.3 — Product Governance & Lifecycle Management

**Business Domain:** BD-15 Distribution, Product & Client Management (Commercial) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Governs the firm's products across their life — the product-approval process, the periodic product review, and the rationalisation of the range. SD-15.3 owns the *product-governance regime*: it operates the product-approval gate every new or materially changed product passes through, defines and reviews each product's target market, runs the periodic reviews that test whether a product still delivers value, and manages the end of a product's life — mergers, closures, share-class changes. It exists because a product is a regulated, ongoing obligation, not a one-off launch: the MiFID II product-governance regime and the UK Consumer Duty make the manufacturer answerable for the product through its life. It is **not** the *building* of the product (SD-15.2) and it is not investment-compliance monitoring of the portfolio (BD-10).

## Service Operations

- **Operate the product-approval process** — run the product-approval and product-governance gate every new or materially changed product passes through.
- **Define and review the target market** — set and periodically review each product's target market and distribution strategy.
- **Run periodic product review** — run the periodic product reviews that test whether each product still delivers value to its target market.
- **Manage product rationalisation** — manage fund mergers, closures, liquidations and share-class rationalisation across the range.
- **Govern the product register** — maintain the governed register of the firm's products and their lifecycle state.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the launched products from SD-15.2; performance and value data from BD-09 and SD-12.11; the product-governance regulation; distribution and flow data from SD-15.4.
- **Outputs:** product approvals, target-market definitions, product-review outcomes and rationalisation decisions — consumed by SD-15.4 (the distribution strategy), the front office and the firm's governance.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** PM-01 Fund & Vehicle, PM-10 Fund Terms; performance and value data from BD-09 and SD-12.11; the SD-15.2 launched products; the SD-15.4 distribution and flow data.
- **Owns:** the product register and the products' lifecycle state — a process artefact.
- **Open question:** a **Product** entity — the firm's offering as a versioned, owned, governed record — anchored in SD-15.3 and shared across the product cluster.

## Standards

- The **MiFID II product-governance** regime — the manufacturer obligations and the target-market regime.
- The **UK Consumer Duty** products-and-services and price-and-value outcomes.
- The **PRIIPs KID** and the UCITS KIID.

- A **Product** entity.
- The product-review and value-assessment sub-model.
