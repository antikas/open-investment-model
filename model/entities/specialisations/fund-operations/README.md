# Fund-Operations Specialisation

**Maturity:** Provisional · twelve entities — fund-product (FO-01), share/unit-class (FO-02), investor unitholding (FO-03), dealing order (FO-04), fund distribution event (FO-05), fee accrual (FO-06), investor tax statement (FO-07), service-provider appointment (FO-08), omnibus account (FO-09), ETF creation/redemption order (FO-10), ETF creation basket / portfolio composition file (FO-11), and ETF authorised-participant agreement (FO-12); the class-grain E-07 Valuation record carries the struck NAV per unit and units in issue

The entity specialisation for the **fund-operator / issued-product form** — the shape an investment holding takes when an institution *issues and operates* a fund rather than investing into one from the outside. An asset manager, a fund sponsor, or a management company that manufactures, registers and runs collective investment vehicles inhabits this form.

This pack is organised by **form of operation**, not by asset class — it is the issued-fund / fund-operator form, orthogonal to the [E-09 asset-class taxonomy](../../core/E-09-asset-class.md) (a UCITS equity fund, a fixed-income SICAV, a money-market fund, an ETF, a private-equity fund run by a GP/ManCo, and a hedge fund are all instances of the issued-product form). The form-of-operation membership is also orthogonal to the `private-markets/` pack: a firm that operates funds appears in this pack from the issuer's perspective; if that same firm also invests as an LP into other managers' funds, its investor-side commitments are private-markets entities.

The five specialisation packs together cover the full spectrum of forms:

| Pack | Form |
|---|---|
| `public-markets/` | The listed-securities form of *holding* |
| **`fund-operations/`** | The issued-fund / fund-operator form — the manager *manufactures* the product |
| `private-markets/` | The closed-end-fund-vehicle / direct-private-credit form of *holding* |
| `derivatives/` | The derivative-instrument form |
| `real-assets/` | The directly-held physical-asset form |

## How this pack specialises the core

Every entity here specialises a [core entity](../../core/). The core says what is universal; this pack adds what is specific to the fund-issuing, fund-operating form:

- An **issued fund** is an Instrument / Asset (E-02) — it is a collective investment vehicle with an identifier (ISIN, SEDOL), a legal structure, a regulatory wrapper, a dealing frequency and a NAV. **FO-01 Fund Product** specifies the issued fund from the manager's side: the product the manager *manufactures*, registers, prices and distributes.
- A **share or unit class** is also an Instrument / Asset (E-02) — the dealing and pricing grain within the fund. Each class carries its own ISIN, fee schedule, distribution policy, currency and hedged-class configuration. **FO-02 Share / Unit Class** is the static class reference record: the authoritative configuration the fund accounting, transfer agency and investor-reporting functions attach to when striking class-level NAV, processing dealing orders and disclosing fees.

What this pack adds that the core does not have: the product-lifecycle attributes (legal structure, regulatory wrapper, authorisation status), the dealing-terms governance parameters (dealing frequency, cut-off, pricing basis, anti-dilution controls), the issued-product identification surface (LEI, external identifiers as a declared read-cache), and the class-grain static reference record (per-class fee schedule, distribution policy, currency, hedged-class flag, investor eligibility). The class-grain E-07 Valuation record (produced by SD-12.9 at the NAV strike) carries units in issue and the struck NAV at class grain. FO-03 Investor Unitholding carries the per-investor, per-class register position; FO-04 Dealing Order is the immutable subscription / redemption / transfer / switch event; FO-05 Fund Distribution Event is the income distribution declaration and lifecycle. The per-period hedge P&L operating state is produced by SD-11.3 each NAV cycle and feeds the SD-12.9 class-NAV strike; fee-accrual is built as FO-06 Fee Accrual, owned by SD-12.11.

## Scope of this pack

This pack models the **fund as an issued product**, the **share or unit class** within it, the **investor's holding of units**, the **dealing events** that move the register, the **fund distribution** the manager pays to its unitholders, the **computed fee figure** that makes the OCF/TER reconstructable, the **issued investor tax statement** of record, the **service-provider appointment** record that binds each fund to its appointed ecosystem of custodians, depositaries, administrators, transfer agents, trustees, auditors, prime brokers and investment managers, and the **omnibus account** relationship recording the account a distributor, platform or sub-transfer agent holds at the fund's register along with the servicing fee terms the manager pays for investor servicing within the omnibus, and the **ETF primary-market dealing path**: the **ETF creation/redemption order** (FO-10, the primary-market in-specie dealing event between the fund and an authorised participant), the **ETF creation basket** (FO-11, the daily Portfolio Composition File specifying the securities and cash that constitute one creation unit), and the **ETF authorised-participant agreement** (FO-12, the standing dated access right of a broker-dealer or market-maker to create and redeem ETF creation units against a specific fund). It does not model:

- The NAV-per-unit figure as a standalone entity — the struck value at class grain is carried as a typed class-grain E-07 Valuation record (produced by SD-12.9), not a separate entity.
- DC recordkeeping at participant grain — excluded by design; see the DC-exclusion section below.

## DC-recordkeeping: deliberately out of scope

A fund manager that offers **defined-contribution retirement recordkeeping** as a service line keeps *participant-level* accounts and investment elections. That service is distinct from the fund's transfer agency: the transfer agent sees the plan-level omnibus position; the recordkeeper maintains participant-level accounts and statements.

OpenIM deliberately excludes participant-level DC recordkeeping from this pack. The transfer agent's plan-level omnibus position is visible in FO-03 Investor Unitholding (the per-investor, per-class register record maintained by SD-12.15 Transfer Agency & Investor Dealing); the participant layer below it is a distinct recordkeeping service line. Industry sources confirm the distinction: the transfer agent holds the plan-level omnibus account; the recordkeeper (a separate function or firm) holds participant accounts, investment elections and statements. A fund manager offering DC recordkeeping is offering a different product from fund transfer agency — one that sits outside the fund-product perimeter this pack models.

This exclusion is sourced. The distinction is well-documented in the retirement-plan-services industry, where the terms of service for transfer agency and recordkeeping are separate regulated functions. OpenIM's fund-operations scope ends at the fund's register of legal unitholders (the omnibus positions the intermediaries / plans hold); it does not extend into participant accounts within those omnibus positions.

Carries the ManCo/AIFM delegation edge (delegates_to_entity_id → the investment manager). Owned by SD-17.8 Vendor, Outsourcing & Service-Provider Oversight; consumed by SD-12.5, SD-12.9, SD-12.15, and SD-16.1. |
| FO-09 | [Omnibus Account](FO-09-omnibus-account.md) | E-25 Account | The registered account relationship between the fund and each distributor, platform or sub-transfer agent that holds units in omnibus — the fund sees one position; the intermediary holds the per-investor accounts. States the omnibus look-through gap as the figure-of-record boundary: the fund's register ends at the omnibus account. Owned by SD-15.4 Distribution Strategy & Channel Management; consumed by SD-12.11, SD-12.15, and SD-12.9. |
| FO-10 | [ETF Creation/Redemption Order](FO-10-etf-creation-redemption-order.md) | E-05 Transaction | The primary-market dealing event in which an authorised participant creates or redeems ETF creation units by exchanging an in-kind basket of underlying securities (FO-11) for fund shares, or the reverse. Distinct from FO-04 (secondary-market cash investor dealing at the transfer-agent window) and PB-03 (exchange order book). Owned by SD-12.15 Transfer Agency & Investor Dealing; consumed by SD-12.1, SD-12.2, SD-12.9, SD-12.10, and SD-14.3. |

Owned by SD-12.15 Transfer Agency & Investor Dealing; consumed by SD-12.1, SD-14.3. |
