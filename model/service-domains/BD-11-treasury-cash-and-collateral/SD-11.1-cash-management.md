# SD-11.1 — Cash Management

**Business Domain:** BD-11 Treasury, Cash & Collateral (Middle office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Knows and positions the cash of every fund and portfolio under management — across every account and every currency — and puts idle cash to work. SD-11.1 is the present-tense treasury capability: it answers *where is the cash, today*, runs the sweeps and nostro movements that keep it positioned, funds the day's settlements, and places surplus balances so cash earns rather than sits idle. Modern buy-side cash management is alpha-aware — cash is an asset with a yield, not only a settlement balance. It is **not** the *forecasting* of future liquidity needs — that is SD-11.2 — and it does not own the booking and reconciliation of cash, which are SD-12.2 and SD-12.10's; SD-11.1 makes the cash decision and instruction, operations records it.

## Service Operations

- **Position cash** — maintain the real-time view of cash across every account, custodian and currency for the funds and portfolios.
- **Manage sweeps and nostro accounts** — run the sweep structures that concentrate and mobilise cash, and oversee the nostro accounts.
- **Fund the day's settlements** — ensure cash is in the right account and currency to meet the day's trade settlements and obligations.
- **Place surplus cash** — invest idle balances — money-market funds, short-dated instruments, deposits — so cash earns a return within the firm's cash-investment policy. This places operational cash, including into money-market funds as a vehicle; the running of a cash / money-market allocation sleeve or a money-market product is SD-05.13 Cash & Money-Market Portfolio Management's.
- **Instruct cash movements** — issue the payment and transfer instructions that move cash, on the standard rails.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the IBOR cash position from SD-12.1; settlement obligations from SD-12.4; income and distribution receipts from SD-12.7; the cash-investment policy; bank and custodian cash statements.
- **Outputs:** the positioned cash record and cash-movement instructions — consumed by SD-12.4 Trade Settlement (the settlement cash), SD-11.2 Liquidity Management (the starting position for the forecast), and SD-12.10 Reconciliation.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-06 Cash Flow Event, E-04 Holding / Position (`book = ibor` — the live cash positions from the IBOR), E-03 Portfolio / Mandate, E-25 Account (`account_type = cash` — the bank-account side, owned by SD-11.7); the SD-12.1 IBOR cash position; the SD-12.4 settlement obligations; the SD-12.7 income and distribution receipts.
- **Owns:** none as a master entity — the cash position is a view over E-04 and E-06. The cash-account structure is E-25 Account on its `cash` partition, owned by SD-11.7 Bank Account & Mandate Administration.

## Standards

- **ISO 20022** cash messaging (the `camt` and `pain` message families) and the SWIFT rails for payment and nostro reporting.
- **SEC Rule 2a-7** and the **EU Money Market Fund Regulation**, where surplus cash is placed in money-market funds.
