# SD-06.6 — Derivatives & OTC Trade Management

**Business Domain:** BD-06 Trading & Execution (Front office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Executes OTC derivatives and manages the clearing path that distinguishes a derivative trade from a cash-instrument trade. A derivative does not simply execute and settle: it is executed by request-for-quote on a swap-execution facility or by voice, it may be routed to central clearing, it is given up to a clearing broker, and it sits under an ISDA master agreement. SD-06.6 owns that OTC-and-clearing path. Listed-derivative execution on an exchange is part of SD-06.2's order-driven mode; SD-06.6 owns what is specific to the OTC and cleared world.

## Service Operations

- **Execute OTC derivatives** — request-for-quote on a swap-execution facility or organised trading facility, or by voice.
- **Determine clearing eligibility and route** — decide whether a trade is centrally cleared and route it to the clearing broker.
- **Process give-ups** — give the executed trade up to the clearing broker that will clear it.
- **Reference the ISDA master agreement** — confirm the trade falls under the master agreement and its credit support annex.
- **Set up margin at execution** — initiate the initial- and variation-margin arrangement, handed to BD-11 for management.
- **Determine the regulatory reporting obligation** — establish the trade-reporting requirement the trade carries.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the derivative orders from SD-06.1; swap-execution-facility and OTF connectivity; the clearing arrangements; the ISDA agreements.
- **Outputs:** the executed derivative trades, the clearing instructions and the regulatory reports — consumed by **BD-12** (clearing processing and settlement) and **BD-11** (margin and collateral).

## Entities

- **Consumes:** the derivatives specialisation entities — Listed Derivative, OTC Derivative (DR-02), Master Agreement (DR-03) — Legal Entity (E-01, counterparties and clearing brokers); the SD-06.1 derivative orders.
- **Owns:** the execution facet of the OTC Derivative (DR-02); the post-trade lifecycle of the contract is owned by SD-12.14.

## Standards

- **Dodd-Frank** (the swap-execution-facility and central-clearing mandates), **EMIR** (clearing, reporting, risk mitigation), **MiFIR** (the trading obligation), and the **ISDA** master-agreement framework. OpenIM positions ISDA CDM as the complementary transaction-layer standard below the model.

## Open extensions

- The boundary with SD-06.2 — listed-derivative execution is SD-06.2's order-driven mode; SD-06.6 owns the OTC and clearing-path specifics.
- The boundary with SD-12.14 Derivatives Lifecycle Processing — SD-06.6 brings the contract into being; SD-12.14 processes its post-trade life.
