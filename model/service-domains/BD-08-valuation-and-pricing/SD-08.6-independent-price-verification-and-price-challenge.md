# SD-08.6 — Independent Price Verification & Price Challenge

**Business Domain:** BD-08 Valuation & Pricing (Middle office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Independently verifies that the prices and valuations the firm is using are right — by checking them against sources independent of the desk and the model that produced them — and runs the formal challenge of contested marks. **Independent Price Verification (IPV)** is a recognised control: a periodic, independent check that the front-office or model marks agree with externally sourced prices, distinct from the daily price-tolerance checks inside SD-08.1. SD-08.6 is the **independent verification layer** — it does not produce valuations, it tests the ones the other Service Domains produce, and it escalates unresolved differences to the SD-08.4 pricing committee. It is not the daily price-tolerance check of SD-08.1 (which is the producer checking its own input) and it is not records reconciliation (SD-12.10, which reconciles positions and cash, not prices).

## Service Operations

- **Run independent price verification** — periodically verify the firm's marks against independent price sources — a second vendor, broker quotes, consensus-pricing services — and quantify and explain the differences.
- **Verify model valuations independently** — test SD-08.2's model marks against independent prices and independent model recalculation, the valuation side of model control.
- **Challenge counterparty and broker marks** — compare the firm's valuations against counterparty statements and margin-call valuations, and run the formal dispute where they differ — the collateral-dispute path SD-11.4 also touches.
- **Run consensus-pricing comparison** — submit to and consume consensus-pricing services (Markit Totem and equivalents) to benchmark hard-to-value marks against the market's mid.
- **Produce the IPV report and exception log** — report the verification coverage, the exceptions found and the unresolved differences, and escalate the material ones to SD-08.4.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the firm's valuations from SD-08.1, SD-08.2 and SD-08.3; independent price sources, broker quotes and consensus-pricing data; counterparty and margin valuations; the IPV policy and thresholds from SD-08.4.
- **Outputs:** IPV results, the verification exception log and price-challenge / dispute outcomes — consumed by SD-08.4 (the pricing committee), SD-12.9 Fund Accounting & NAV, SD-14.8 Internal Audit and SD-14.7 Internal Control & Assurance.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-07 Valuation (any `method` — IPV runs across the observable-price, mark-to-model and manager-mark / appraisal valuations), E-08 Price & Market Data, E-02 Instrument / Asset, and the counterparty-collateral entity DR-04 Margin & Collateral Balance; the SD-08.1 firm's valuations; the SD-08.2 firm's valuations; the SD-08.3 firm's valuations; the SD-08.4 IPV policy and thresholds.
- **Owns:** none — IPV is a control over valuations other Service Domains own; it produces verification findings, not a master valuation.
- **Open question:** whether a **Price-Verification Exception** entity is warranted — a recorded, aged verification difference with a resolution lifecycle, parallel in shape to the Reconciliation Break candidate (SD-12.10) and to E-18 Limit Breach. An exception that carries provenance, ageing and a resolution trail is auditable in a way a transient finding is not.

## Standards

- **Independent price verification as a recognised control** — the Basel Committee's prudent-valuation guidance names independent price verification as a key valuation control, distinct from daily marking.
- The EU **prudent-valuation** regime and **IFRS 13**, which assume an independent verification process behind a reported fair value.
- **Consensus-pricing services** (Markit Totem and equivalents) as the external benchmark for hard-to-value instruments.

## Open extensions

- The **Price-Verification Exception** entity — the open question above.
- The IPV coverage and threshold sub-model — which instruments are verified, how often, and against what tolerance.
- The Service-Operation-level input/output contracts.
