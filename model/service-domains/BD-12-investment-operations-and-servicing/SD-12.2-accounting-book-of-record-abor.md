# SD-12.2 — Accounting Book of Record (ABOR)

**Business Domain:** BD-12 Investment Operations & Servicing (Back office) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Maintains the official, accounting-basis book of holdings and balances — the authoritative ledger from which net asset value, financial reporting and audit are produced. Where the IBOR (SD-12.1) is the real-time view the front office trades against, the ABOR is the *official record*: positions and balances kept on accounting rules, posted to a general ledger, and struck at a periodic book close. It exists because the firm's official numbers must rest on a controlled, accounting-disciplined book — not the intraday trading view, which is correct for trading but not struck for reporting. It is **not** the IBOR, and it is not fund-level NAV striking — that is SD-12.9, which maintains the books of funds the institution *operates*; SD-12.2 maintains the accounting book of the institution's own portfolios.

## Service Operations

- **Accounting-basis position keeping** — maintain holdings and balances on the `abor` book, recognised and measured on the firm's accounting policy, distinct from the intraday IBOR position.
- **General-ledger posting** — translate each transaction and event into the corresponding accounting entries and post them to the investment general ledger.
- **Income and expense accrual** — accrue interest, dividends, fees and expenses into the accounting book between cash dates, so the book reflects earned-but-unreceived and incurred-but-unpaid amounts.
- **Cost-basis and lot accounting** — maintain the cost basis of each position and, where required, the tax-lot detail, so realised and unrealised gain is correctly measured.
- **Trade-date / settlement-date accounting** — recognise transactions on the basis the accounting policy specifies, and carry the difference between trade-dated and settlement-dated views.
- **Book close** — run the periodic (daily, monthly, quarterly) close: cut off activity, complete accruals, validate, and lock the accounting book for the period.
- **Adjusting and correcting entries** — post the corrections, reclassifications and restatement entries an accounting book requires, under control and with an audit trail.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** transactions and cash flows from SD-12.1 (E-05, E-06); valuations from SD-08; corporate-action and income outcomes from SD-12.6 and SD-12.7; fee, expense and carry amounts from SD-12.11; reconciliation results from SD-12.10.
- **Outputs:** the accounting-basis book of holdings and balances, and the investment general ledger — consumed by SD-12.9 Fund Accounting & NAV, SD-09 Performance & Analytics, SD-16.4 Financial Reporting & Disclosure, SD-17.1 Corporate Accounting & Controllership, SD-17.4 Investment & Portfolio Tax, and SD-14.8 Internal Audit.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-05 Transaction, E-06 Cash Flow Event, E-07 Valuation (any `method` — ABOR applies the official accounting valuation method per fair-value-governance policy), E-03 Portfolio / Mandate; the SD-12.1 transactions and cash flows; the SD-12.10 reconciliation results; the SD-12.11 fee, expense and carry amounts; the SD-12.6 corporate-action and income outcomes; the SD-12.7 corporate-action and income outcomes.
- **Owns:** **E-04 Holding / Position** on the `abor` book — SD-12.2 is the authoritative source for the accounting-basis position. E-04 carries a `book` discriminator and an `accrued_income_usd` attribute specifically for the ABOR book. The general ledger itself is an accounting structure; whether OpenIM models a ledger-account entity is an open question — see below.
- **Open question:** whether the investment general ledger warrants its own entity (a chart-of-accounts and posting structure) rather than being treated as downstream of E-04 / E-05. Flagged for the open-extensions pass alongside the IBOR / ABOR reconciliation model.

## Standards

- **IFRS / local GAAP** — the accounting standards the book is kept to; IFRS 9 governs the classification and measurement of financial instruments, IFRS 13 fair-value measurement.
- ISO 20022 messaging carries the transaction and event data the ledger is posted from.
- The IBOR / ABOR distinction is a recognised buy-side operating-model pattern, reflected in the platform vendor stacks; the ABOR is the side those stacks built their fund-accounting modules around.
