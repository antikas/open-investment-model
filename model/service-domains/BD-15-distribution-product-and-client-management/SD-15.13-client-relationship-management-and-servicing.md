# SD-15.13 — Client Relationship Management & Servicing

**Business Domain:** BD-15 Distribution, Product & Client Management (Commercial) · **Applies:** BOTH

## Purpose

Manages the ongoing relationship with the firm's clients and investors — keeping them served, satisfied and retained. SD-15.13 owns the client relationship after onboarding: it is the relationship-management function, it handles client and investor queries and requests, it manages the client experience, and it works client retention. It exists because winning a client is not keeping one — the ongoing relationship is where assets are retained or lost. It is **not** the onboarding (SD-15.11) and it is not the commercial reporting to the client — that is SD-15.14, though the two work closely.

## Service Operations

- **Manage the client relationship** — own and manage the ongoing relationship with each client and investor — the relationship plan and the contact cadence.
- **Handle client queries and requests** — receive and resolve client and investor queries, requests and instructions.
- **Manage the client experience** — manage the quality of the client experience across every touchpoint.
- **Work client retention** — monitor client satisfaction and at-risk relationships, and work retention.
- **Manage upsell and cross-sell** — identify and work the opportunities to deepen an existing client relationship.

## Inputs and outputs

- **Inputs:** the onboarded client from SD-15.11; client queries and feedback; the client and investor reporting from SD-15.14; relationship and satisfaction data.
- **Outputs:** the managed client relationship and the retention and cross-sell signals — consumed by SD-15.7 (the cross-sell opportunities) and the firm's governance.

## Entities

- **Consumes:** E-01 Legal Entity (the client / investor), E-03 Portfolio / Mandate; the SD-15.11 onboarded client; the SD-15.14 client and investor reporting.
- **Owns:** the client-relationship record — a process artefact; part of the **Client / Investor** entity.

## Standards

- No single external standard governs relationship management; the **UK Consumer Duty** consumer-support outcome bears on client servicing.

## Open extensions
